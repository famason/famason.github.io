Plant Life

There are 5 groups of plants in the Pacific Ocean

Phytoplankton

Also known as microalgae, this microsopic marine algae contains chlorophyll and survive off of sunlight. It accounts for about half of the photosynthesis on the planet and is a main food source for many species of animals.

Important Nutrients for Phytoplankton to Survive
Phosphate
Nitrate
Silicate
Calcium
Sulfur

Red Algae

A eukaryotic group of algae that contains over 7,000 species. Some types of red algae produce harmful toxins that kill fish and other creatures.

Most Common Red Algae in the Pacific Ocean
Mastocarpus Papillatus

Kelp

Residing in waters near shore, kelp grows in dense groups that provide shelter and food for thousands of animal species including fish, aquatic mammals, and invertebrates.

Kelp is primarily consumed by...
Sea Urchins

Seagrass

It cleans water, and it also takes CO2 out of the atmosphere making it the third most valuable ecosystem in the world.

In the whole world
Seagrass covers around 300,000 km squared
and is present in 159 countries

Sargassum

A large group of brown algae that is mostly eaten by turtle, shrimp, and seabirds.

Rich in...
Nitrogen
Phosphorus
Potassium
